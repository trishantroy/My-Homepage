Trishant - Projects

Third Year Undergraduate
Indian Institute of Technology Bombay

I have been a member of a technical team, Unmesh Mashruwala Innovation Cell (UMIC) since my first year. Robotics is a field with piques my interest and UMIC has given a great opportunity for working on projects associated to Robotics.

Since July'17, I have been working in a team of 12 students to tackle the challenging problem statement of the International Aerial Robotics Competition (IARC). We are designing Autonomous Quadcopters with human interaction and swarm interaction in a GPS-denied indoor environment. IARC is the longest running collegiate aerial robotics challenge in the world and is managed by the Association for Unmanned Vehicle Systems International Foundation (AUVSI).

International Aerial Robotics Competition (IARC)

Guide: Prof. Vivek Sangwan, Department of Mechanical Enginnering, IIT Bombay

  • Responsible for Controls sub-system in the 12-member team designing Autonomous Quadcopters capable of man-machine and swarm interaction in a GPS-denied indoor environment

  • Analyzed, designed and fabricated the electronic platform for the autonomous quadcopters

  • Implemented remote control of pose and orientation of multiple quadcopters on ROS platform

  • Implemented PID control for stabilization of 4 quadcopters and following dynamic paths

  • Explored data and real-time video transfer using serial communication via radio telemetry

American Society for Mechanical Engineers (ASME) - Student Design Challenge

Guide: Prof. Abhishek Gupta, Department of Mechanical Enginnering, IIT Bombay

  • Part of 16-member team to ideate and build a Multi-Functional Mechatronic Vehicle

  • Completed specified tasks, namely Sprint, Lift, Hit, Climb and Throw within given constraints

  • Designed a Lead Screw Mechanism to lift weight of 30 kg to a height 30 cm above top end of the bot

  • Won all the 5 events with a huge margin against Asia Pacific teams, to advance to the World Finals
