Trishant - Projects

Third Year Undergraduate
Department of Energy Science and Engineering
Indian Institute of Technology Bombay

I have been a member of the technical team, Unmesh Mashruwala Innovation Cell (UMIC) since my first year. Robotics is a field with piques my interest and UMIC has given a great opportunity for working on projects associated to Robotics.

Since the past one year, I have been working to design Autonomous Quadcopters capable of human and swarm interaction in a GPS-denied indoor environment to tackle the challenging problem statement in the International Aerial Robotics Competition (IARC). It is the longest running collegiate aerial robotics challenge in the world and is managed by the Association for Unmanned Vehicle Systems International Foundation (AUVSI).

I am primarily working under Prof. Leena Vachhani on development of a spherical robot sensors that can be used
to explore unknown indoor enviroments. Essentially, my current task is to ensure sturdy and stable motion of the
robot by developing an apt control system. I am also guided by Prof. Dipti Gupta. Under her guidance, I am working to
select a suitable material which when used to fabricate the robot body will enable us to throw it in desired environments
without damaging it. This is my Bachelor's Degree project and requires me to do a thorough literature survey before
conducting relevant experiments in lab. The experimentation typically involves motion analysis on Viacom Motion
Capture Systems and tensile strength analysis on Universal Testing Machine.

Using my knowledge of ROS and Gazebo, I am working with the Institute Autonomous Underwater Vehicle team
to make the simulations more realistic with respect to the working environment in order to improve testing time.
